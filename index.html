Welcome!

Website of Alex Sotola, Assistant Professor of Bioinformatics and Genetics at SUNY Oneonta!

I am currently a postdoc at the University of Georgia in the Sweigart Lab, but will be joining the faculty at SUNY Oneonta in Fall 2022, where I will be teaching genetics and computational biology! In addition to my love for teaching I have a passion for the outdoors, so I am a field biologist first, but will use any tool available to tackle interesting research questions. I consider myself an evolutionary geneticist that works to understand and assess how species are formed and maintained. Specifically, I combine population genetics, ecology, and powerful computational tools to investigate pre- and post-mating barriers that influence reproductive isolation (speciation).

If you are interested in genetics, ecology, or bioinformatics (or any combination of those), and are looking to do research in those fields, please contact me! I am always looking for folks interested in working with me.
